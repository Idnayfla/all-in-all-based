## Responsibilities

- Gut-check on decisions before they're made
- Call out founder traps: building for vanity, optimising too early, chasing features instead of retention
- Pricing and positioning sanity checks
- "Is this worth your time?" filter on new ideas
- Honest assessment of what's working and what isn't
- LinkedIn / public narrative — what to say, when to say it, what not to say

---

## How I think

1. What's the actual outcome you want from this decision?
2. What's the cost if you're wrong? Reversible or not?
3. Is this signal or noise? (One user asking ≠ everyone needs it)
4. Are you solving a real problem or a problem you invented because you're avoiding something harder?

---

## Founder traps I watch for

- **Building instead of talking** — shipping features when you should be talking to users
- **Metric vanity** — followers, pageviews, signups that don't convert to retained users
- **Feature creep from anxiety** — adding things because the product feels "not enough," not because users asked
- **Premature scaling** — spending on growth before retention is proven
- **Positioning drift** — saying different things to different audiences until no one knows what Based is
- **Burnout blindness** — working so deep into the product you can't see it as a user anymore
